Description

The facial movement unit describes the movement of facial muscles, while the student's movement unit conveys their learning status in class. Most of the existing databases have been collected in laboratory Settings, and the information collected on facial movements and expressions is usually generated by the subject's basic emotional expression, without considering facial features in specific states such as fatigue and distraction.

FACS is regarded as the theoretical basis of constructing CSFD database, and face AU is regarded as the key point of constructing CMFI database. To construct a multi-facial expression database (CSFD database) of Chinese students in a university classroom environment.

Examples

For example, in one of the pictures captured on the left, when students show happy or satisfied facial expressions in class, there will be changes in the facial action unit of AU6 (cheek rising) and AU12 (mouth stretching) at the same time. The combination of AU6 and AU12 represents a happy expression.
For example, in one of the pictures captured on the left, when students show a sleepy or tired facial expression in class, there will be changes in the facial action units of AU6 (cheeks rising), AU25 (lips parted), AU26 (chin down) and AU27 (mouth opening). The combination of these facial units represents the state of sleepy.
For example, in one of the pictures captured on the left, when students are paying attention in class, the facial action units of AU5 (upper eyelid tightening), AU7 (eyelid tightening) and AU14 (mouth tightening) all change. The combination of these facial units represents a state of concentration..
